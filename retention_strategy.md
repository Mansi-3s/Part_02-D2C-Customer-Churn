# D2C Customer Retention Strategy

## Executive Summary

This report presents a customer retention strategy based on RFM (Recency, Frequency, Monetary) segmentation combined with customer support, engagement, and discount-behavior signals. The objective is to identify high-value customers, customers at risk of churn, and customer groups requiring targeted retention interventions.

A total of 2,400 customers were segmented into six business-focused groups:

| Segment                | Customers |
| ---------------------- | --------: |
| Dormant Customers      |     1,036 |
| Loyal Customers        |       536 |
| Discount Sensitive     |       326 |
| Champions              |       285 |
| High Value But Unhappy |       140 |
| At Risk                |        77 |

---

# 1. Champions

## Customer Behavior

Champions are the most valuable customers. They purchase frequently, spend significantly, and remain actively engaged with the platform. These customers have strong RFM scores and consistently interact with the website or app.

## Why They Matter

Champions generate substantial revenue and often become brand advocates. Losing even a small number of these customers can have a significant impact on revenue and customer lifetime value.

## Recommended Retention Action

* VIP loyalty programs
* Early access to new products
* Exclusive member benefits
* Premium customer service
* Referral reward programs

## Expected Business Value

* Increased customer lifetime value
* Higher repeat purchase rates
* Organic customer acquisition through referrals

---

# 2. Loyal Customers

## Customer Behavior

Loyal Customers purchase regularly and maintain strong engagement with the brand. While they may not spend as much as Champions, they form the core revenue base of the business.

## Why They Matter

This segment represents a large proportion of active customers and contributes stable recurring revenue.

## Recommended Retention Action

* Loyalty points programs
* Personalized recommendations
* Cross-selling and upselling campaigns
* Referral incentives

## Expected Business Value

* Increased purchase frequency
* Higher average order value
* Improved long-term retention

---

# 3. High Value But Unhappy

## Customer Behavior

These customers have above-average spending levels but have experienced repeated customer service issues, complaints, or negative support interactions.

## Why They Matter

This is one of the highest-risk segments because these customers contribute meaningful revenue but are showing dissatisfaction signals. Losing them would result in both revenue loss and potential negative word-of-mouth.

## Recommended Retention Action

* Dedicated support outreach
* Service recovery programs
* Personalized customer success follow-up
* Complaint resolution within priority SLA
* Recovery vouchers or goodwill credits

## Expected Business Value

* Prevention of high-value customer churn
* Improved customer satisfaction
* Reduced negative sentiment

---

# 4. Discount Sensitive Customers

## Customer Behavior

These customers primarily purchase when discounts or promotional offers are available. Their buying decisions are strongly influenced by pricing incentives.

## Why They Matter

Although margins may be lower, this segment contributes incremental revenue during promotional campaigns.

## Recommended Retention Action

* Personalized coupon campaigns
* Bundle offers
* Seasonal promotions
* Targeted discount recommendations

## Expected Business Value

* Increased campaign conversion rates
* Higher promotional revenue
* Better utilization of marketing campaigns

---

# 5. At Risk Customers

## Customer Behavior

At Risk customers were previously valuable but have become inactive in recent months. Their purchase recency is high despite strong historical spending.

## Why They Matter

This segment has already demonstrated willingness to spend and therefore offers the highest potential return from retention efforts.

## Recommended Retention Action

* Personalized win-back campaigns
* Reactivation offers
* Email and SMS reminders
* Product recommendations based on past purchases
* Time-limited incentives

## Expected Business Value

* Recovery of previously valuable customers
* Reduced churn rate
* Significant revenue preservation

---

# 6. Dormant Customers

## Customer Behavior

Dormant Customers have low engagement, low purchase frequency, and limited recent activity. This is the largest segment in the customer base.

## Why They Matter

While individual customer value is relatively low, the segment represents a large pool of potential reactivation opportunities.

## Recommended Retention Action

* Low-cost email campaigns
* Re-engagement surveys
* Welcome-back offers
* Content-driven engagement campaigns

## Expected Business Value

* Incremental revenue gains
* Improved customer database utilization
* Better understanding of disengagement reasons

---

# Campaign Budget Prioritization

Assuming a retention budget of ₹100,000:

| Segment                | Allocation |  Budget |
| ---------------------- | ---------: | ------: |
| At Risk                |        40% | ₹40,000 |
| Loyal Customers        |        25% | ₹25,000 |
| Champions              |        15% | ₹15,000 |
| High Value But Unhappy |        10% | ₹10,000 |
| Discount Sensitive     |         5% |  ₹5,000 |
| Dormant Customers      |         5% |  ₹5,000 |

---

# Why At Risk Customers Are Prioritized First

At Risk customers receive the largest budget allocation because:

1. They have already demonstrated strong historical spending behavior.
2. Their recent inactivity indicates elevated churn probability.
3. Retaining an existing customer is significantly cheaper than acquiring a new customer.
4. Recovering even a small portion of this segment can generate substantial revenue impact.

This segment offers the highest expected return on retention investment.

---

# Expected Business Impact

The proposed retention strategy is expected to:

* Reduce customer churn.
* Increase customer lifetime value.
* Improve customer satisfaction.
* Strengthen loyalty among high-value customers.
* Improve marketing ROI through targeted interventions.
* Preserve revenue from high-value customer segments.

---

# Conclusion

The segmentation framework combines RFM analysis with customer engagement, support, and discount behavior signals to create actionable customer groups. The strategy prioritizes At Risk and High Value But Unhappy customers while maintaining engagement among Champions and Loyal Customers. This targeted approach allows the business to maximize retention effectiveness and allocate marketing resources efficiently.
